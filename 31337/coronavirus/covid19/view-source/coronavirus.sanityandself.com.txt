Do not attach it to any ethnicity or nationality, or social class. <strong>Be empathetic</strong> to all those who are affected, in and from any country. People who are affected by Covid-19 have not done anything wrong, and they deserve our support, compassion and kindness.</p></div></div></div><div class="col sqs-col-4 span-4"><div class="sqs-block html-block sqs-block-html" data-block-type="2" id="block-849b6d912018b4194b41"><div class="sqs-block-content"><p class="sqsrte-large" style="white-space:pre-wrap;"><strong>Say no to stigma </strong></p><p class="sqsrte-small" style="white-space:pre-wrap;">Do not refer to people with the disease as “COVID-19 cases”, “victims” “COVID-19 families” or the “diseased”. They are “people who have COVID-19”, “people who are being treated for COVID19”, “people who are recovering from COVID-19” and after recovering from COVID-19 their life will go on with their jobs, families and loved ones. It is important to separate a person from having an identity defined by COVID-19, to reduce stigma. </p></div></div></div></div><div class="sqs-block spacer-block sqs-block-spacer sized vsize-1" data-block-type="21" id="block-3ee1db6581b1a04d1da2"><div class="sqs-block-content">&nbsp;</div></div><div class="row sqs-row"><div class="col sqs-col-4 span-4"><div class="sqs-block html-block sqs-block-html" data-block-type="2" id="block-a5d194d50ef8ddbd0bf8"><div class="sqs-block-content"><p class="sqsrte-large" style="white-space:pre-wrap;"><strong>Facts minimize fears  </strong></p><p class="sqsrte-small" style="white-space:pre-wrap;">Minimize watching, reading or listening to news that causes you to feel anxious or distressed; seek information only from <strong>trusted sources</strong> and mainly to take practical steps to prepare your plans and protect yourself and loved ones. Seek information updates at specific times during the day, once or twice. The sudden and near-constant stream of news reports about an outbreak can cause anyone to feel worried. </p></div></div></div><div class="col sqs-col-4 span-4"><div class="sqs-block html-block sqs-block-html" data-block-type="2" id="block-a8ed55db33e6de16d672"><div class="sqs-block-content"><p class="sqsrte-large" style="white-space:pre-wrap;"><strong>Support your community</strong></p><p class="sqsrte-small" style="white-space:pre-wrap;">Working together as one community can help to create solidarity in addressing Covid-19 together. Many aspects of our community are suffering from individuals to businesses. Many people are dealing with working from home with loved ones home, others are facing the very real fears of being unable to work from home and struggling with the impacts of limitations that directly affect their finances. Be sensitive, support them when and where you can while maintaining your own social distancing restrictions. Reach out, ask what they need, how you can help and then follow through. If you are affected, take help when it’s offered. We know it’s hard, we know it feels vulnerable, but we all need an extra hand sometimes.</p></div></div></div></div></div></div></div></div></div>

It is quite normal to be feeling this way in the current situation. Stress and the feelings associated with it are by no means a reflection that you cannot do your job or that you are weak. Managing your mental health and psychosocial wellbeing during this time is as important as managing your physical health. </p></div></div><div class="sqs-block spacer-block sqs-block-spacer sized vsize-1" data-block-type="21" id="block-43a95d3ea41f12ac569f"><div class="sqs-block-content">&nbsp;</div></div></div><div class="col sqs-col-4 span-4"><div class="sqs-block html-block sqs-block-html" data-block-type="2" id="block-8295090cc2df08b8e14c"><div class="sqs-block-content"><h3 style="text-align:center;white-space:pre-wrap;"><strong>It’s marathon, not a sprint.</strong></h3><p class="" style="white-space:pre-wrap;"> Take care of <strong>yourself </strong>at this time. Try and use helpful coping strategies such as ensuring sufficient rest during work or between shifts, eat sufficient and healthy food, engage in physical activity, and stay in contact with family and friends. Avoid using unhelpful coping strategies such as tobacco, alcohol or other drugs. In the long term, these can worsen your mental and physical wellbeing. You are most likely to know how to de-stress and you should not be hesitant in keeping yourself psychologically well. This is not a sprint; it’s a marathon.</p></div></div><div class="sqs-block spacer-block sqs-block-spacer sized vsize-1" data-block-type="21" id="block-877b5fc917843d7b3f5a"><div class="sqs-block-content">&nbsp;</div></div></div><div class="col sqs-col-4 span-4"><div class="sqs-block html-block sqs-block-html" data-block-type="2" id="block-3e931e3d36256ed52012"><div class="sqs-block-content"><h3 style="text-align:center;white-space:pre-wrap;"><strong>Stay connected </strong></h3><p class="" style="white-space:pre-wrap;">Some healthcare workers may unfortunately experience avoidance by their family or community due to stigma or fear. This can make an already challenging situation far more difficult. If possible, staying connected with your loved ones including through digital methods is one way to maintain contact. Turn to your colleagues, your manager or other trusted persons for social support- your colleagues may be having similar experiences to you. </p></div></div><div class="sqs-block spacer-block sqs-block-spacer sized vsize-1" data-block-type="21" id="block-9020e7c3aad77e9a06d0"><div class="sqs-block-content">&nbsp;</div></div></div></div><div class="row sqs-row"><div class="col sqs-col-6 span-6"><div class="sqs-block button-block sqs-block-button" data-block-type="53" id="block-13a932c2420c511534b4"><div class="sqs-block-content"><div class="sqs-block-button-container--center" data-animation-role="button" data-alignment="center" data-button-size="large">
