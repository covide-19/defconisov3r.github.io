        <div kjb-settings-id="sections_1584571783270_settings_text" style="text-align: left">
          <p>The COVID-19 pandemic has been one of the most unusual and fluid challenges that any of us have ever faced. The leadership teams here at the International House of Prayer University and the International House of Prayer have been working many hours together to make the best decisions possible for the well-being of our students, interns, and broader missionary staff and spiritual family. As the situation has changed daily, it has not been easy for us to gain clarity. We thank you for your patience with us as we have worked through many different issues and challenges.</p>
<p>Our desire from the beginning was to work together to engage in our educational objectives locally both as an academic institution and a spiritual community pursuing Jesus together. However, this rapidly changing situation has left us with few options but to make the following decisions, which have been made to serve our student body and interns in light of the encroaching and increasing difficulties that lie ahead related to both present and, likely, forthcoming government mandates and restrictions. Secondly, our desire is to ensure that both students and interns are equipped to finish the school year or track successfully while growing spiritually. Finally, things changed so rapidly that what began with a local mandate to avoid all gatherings over a thousand became gatherings of two hundred and fifty, then fifty, and then finally ten or less. Therefore, in order to comply with our governmental authorities and effectively engage in the fight to "flatten the curve" of the rapidly spreading pandemic, we have made some difficult decisions regarding the remainder of our semester and internship tracks.</p>
<p>There are many important questions that will follow this announcement, and we want to hear them all! For the quickest resolution, please remember to direct them to your ministry team coach, worship team coach, or internship leader.</p>
<p><strong>Students:</strong></p>
<p>1. The IHOPU campus is closed, and students are asked to return home by Sunday, March 22, 2020 for the remainder of the school year.</p>
<p>2. We realize that some students have made Kansas City home or have connected in a real, profound way and wish to stay locally and continue to engage with the worship and prayer ministry, specifically the Global Prayer Room. We welcome that, within federal and local guidelines along with, where applicable, parental consent. More information on how to stay local will follow this announcement soon.</p>
<p>3. All fourth quarter FSM, FMA, FMI and Chinese classes are being moved online for remote instruction.</p>
<p>4. This includes the semester-long, skill-based courses that are in progress, as well as the Prayer Room Practicum (worship and ministry teams, in an adapted online format). The online course schedules will be forthcoming, as will an online orientation to ensure everyone is equipped to use IHOPU's&nbsp;interactive online learning software. FMA and FMI students will receive communication from their Program Directors and course instructions regarding skill-based learning requirements.</p>
<p>5. Spring Break is extended by one week with online classes commencing March 30, 2020 at 6pm CT.</p>
<p>6. The Spring Ministry Trips (SMT), in their current form, are cancelled. Further information on SMT will be released within a week.</p>
<p>7. Externs will be receiving communication from their Program Director on how to proceed, and IHOPU will work with each student to make sure they can successfully fulfill their credit requirements.</p>
<p><strong>Interns:</strong></p>
<p>1. The IHOPKC campus is closed, and interns are asked to return home by Sunday, March 22, 2020 for the remainder of the internship track.</p>
<p>2. One Thing Interns will continue their internship remotely in conjunction with the fourth quarter of IHOPU. We will resume your "online internship" on March 30, 2020 at 6pm CT.</p>
<p>3. Intro to IHOPKC, Simeon Company, and Fire in the Night Directors are making arrangements regarding how your internship tracks can be completed remotely. Please pay attention to forthcoming communication to receive more details.</p>
<p>4. Your Internship Director and teams will provide the next steps in regards to course information and answer all of your questions.</p>
<p><strong>What does this mean for IHOPU, Internships, and you?</strong></p>
<p>Wherever we reside during this season, we are a student body and spiritual family, and we are the body of Christ. The end goal hasn't changed: together, we will finish this school year and internship tracks strong. You will gain your credits to graduate or be ready for next fall if you so choose. More importantly, we will continue to reach for God together. For many of you receiving this message, you and your teams will continue to connect and brief throughout each week in online groups, praying together, worshipping together, and reaching for the Lord together. We thank you for your patience and willingness to engage with us in night and day prayer for breakthrough, which is needed now more than any time in recent memory. This is a glorious privilege to serve such an amazing group of students and interns, and we are sure that this is not the end. You are a part of a much larger story now, and I cannot wait to see what unfolds next.&nbsp;</p>
<p>I wrote these words recently, and I believe them fervently: This is an incredible time to be alive, preaching the gospel, and setting our love on Jesus in the midst of the storm. Let the Church serve as a rock of stability without fear, praying fervently for our governmental leaders at every level with no complaint or cynicism, but with faith, hope, and great love.</p>
<p>This is the hour for the light of the beauty of Jesus to shine forth.</p>
<p>Much grace and peace to you in the days ahead,</p>
<p>David Sliker<br />Vice President, International House of Prayer University</p>
