<p><strong>HATTIESBURG, Miss. – (March 16, 2020)&nbsp;</strong><br />Forrest General Hospital and Hattiesburg Clinic have announced the creation of a cough and fever clinic in an effort to stop the coronavirus from spreading. On Wednesday, March 18<sup>th</sup>, the Cloverleaf Immediate Care facility located at 5909 Highway 49 South (located adjacent to Wal-Mart) will be converted to a clinic to assess patients with cough, fever, and other respiratory symptoms.&nbsp;</p>



<ul><li>This walk-in clinic will be open from 8am – 6pm 7 days a week and staffed by physicians. IT IS IMPORTANT, IN ORDER TO HELP US CONTAIN THIS VIRUS, THAT IF PEOPLE DO NOT HAVE THESE SYMPTOMS, THEY CONTINUE TO USE THEIR CURRENT HEALTHCARE PROVIDERS FOR CARE</strong>.</li><li>Patients will be triaged and evaluated, and COVID testing will be done when appropriate based on clinical criteria and guidance for the CDC and Mississippi Department of Health.</li><li>It is open to all patients regardless of age or pre-existing medical conditions, regardless of their ability to pay. <p>As
the first hospital system in the State of Mississippi to diagnose COVID-19
patients, several of our staff have been exposed. Per the CDC guidelines on
3/13/20, we asked staff members who were categorized as a high-risk exposure to
quarantine at home. The decision regarding whom to quarantine is based on
detailed exposure analysis under the direction of the MS State Epidemiologist,
Dr. Paul Byers. Every staff member who had high-risk exposure as of 3/13/20 was
notified.&nbsp; </p>



<p>Since
this time, the CDC regulations have been updated allowing those with high risk
exposure to return to work with certain precautions if they have shown no
symptoms. Many states have already implemented this policy. The following was
communicated today 3/14/20 from Dr. Thomas Dobbs, Mississippi State Health
Officer, noting that after careful consideration and review staff may return to
work with the caveats below:&nbsp; </p>



<ol><li>Report
the onset of fever or respiratory symptoms to supervisor immediately. If these
occur the employee must self-isolate and undergo clinical assessment.</li><li>Maintain
twice daily fever and symptom log (cough, difficulty breathing) at home and
while at work. Fever and symptoms must be checked at start of shift.</li><li>Wear
a surgical mask during work and adhere to strict hand hygiene.</li></ol>



<p>It
is important to note three things:&nbsp; </p>



<ol><li>Exposure
does not mean infection. Those that were exposed will not even be tested unless
they are symptomatic.&nbsp; </li><li>Not
all staff who have been exposed to COVID-19 patients are at risk.&nbsp; </li><li>You
will be working along -side colleagues who will have a mask on. <strong>They are not contagious</strong> to you
nor do you have to fear interaction. This is to take an abundance of caution,
but it does not mean they are infectious.</li></ol>



<p>The
state reported yesterday that there have been 6 presumptive cases. Forrest
Health has cared for two of these patients &#8212; one in an outpatient setting, who
is isolated at home, and one as an inpatient currently in isolation. Additionally,
there is a suspected case at Highland Community Hospital, who is also in
inpatient isolation.</p>



<p>Forrest
General has sent 7 cases to the state for testing with 3 more pending to be tested</p>



<p>on
Monday. Our outlying facilities have sent 5 with 3 more pending to be tested
Monday. We will continue to provide updates as more information becomes
available.</p>



<p>In
this time of heightened alert, it is normal to feel scared. The antidote to
fear is knowledge. The antidote to this COVID-19 pandemic is our resolve to
work together to care for our community.</p>



<p>Thank
you.</p>



<p>Andy
Woodard</p>

<!-- /wp:paragraph --><!-- wp:list -->
<ul>

<!-- /wp:paragraph --><!-- wp:list -->

<li>Use your elbow or a tissue, if available, to cover any coughs or sneezes and then dispose of the tissue.</li>
<li>Stay home or seek medical care if experiencing any symptoms. If you are experiencing symptoms such as fever, cough, shortness of breath and think it may be related to the Coronavirus, please call your primary care provider ahead of time before going to their office location. </li>

<!-- /wp:list --><!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>The first case of COVID-19 in the state was discussed during a press conference Thursday at Forrest General Hospital. The State Department of Health confirmed on Wednesday that a 49-year-old Forrest County male resident had tested positive for the virus.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph --><!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Rambod Rouhbakhsh, MD, Asst. Director Forrest General Family Medicine Residency Program, reported this Forrest County resident had traveled out of the state and began showing signs of fever, cough and muscle aches upon his return to the state. He presented himself for testing at one of Forrest General’s clinics. Tests, both nasal and oral, were obtained and submitted to the State Lab for testing of the Coronavirus.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph --><!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Prior to reporting to the clinic, the patient called ahead and was gloved and masked to help prevent the possibility of spread to patients and healthcare workers. The patient was subsequently sent home for self-quarantine.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph --><!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Late Wednesday, Forrest General Hospital administration was notified by the State Lab that the test was presumptively positive for the Coronavirus and was being sent to the CDC for confirmation.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph --><!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>The patient also traveled with a companion who visited another clinic where the same tests were conducted and submitted.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph --><!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>“We are currently awaiting those test results,” Rouhbakhsh said.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph --><!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>“We want to thank this patient for calling in ahead of time before presenting, and we wish him a speedy recovery,” Rouhbakhsh said. At this time, the patient is reportedly doing well.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph --><!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Forrest General has currently sent three samples to the State Lab for testing. All three have been negative.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph --><!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Per the CDC, the symptoms for Coronavirus are fever, cough and shortness of breath. Similarly, typical symptoms of the flu are cough, congestion and fever. For perspective, in 2018, the hospital treated 1,642 flu cases.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph --><!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>“It is far more likely that patients with these symptoms have an illness other than COVID-19,” said Rouhbakhsh. “Nevertheless we were aware that cases would eventually come to our state. We have been preparing for weeks to be ready to care for our patients. We will get through this.</p>

<p>“We are asking the public to rema0in calm. Due to outbreaks overseas and the national attention, supplies for personal protective equipment are in short supply. These are things like masks, gowns and gloves. Due to this short supply, we will be limiting the number of visitors inside our hospitals. Starting today, we will limit one visitor per patient until such time shipment of supplies starts flowing again. We ask for your patience during this time of heightened alert.”</p>
