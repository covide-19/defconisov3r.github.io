<h1>Covid-19 coronavirus today, news, online distribution map, statistics</h1>

<p>COVID-19 is a new disease that can affect your lungs and Airways. This is caused by a virus called a coronavirus.</p>

<p>A coronavirus is a type of virus. As a group, coronaviruses are common throughout the world. Typical symptoms of coronavirus include fever and cough, which can develop into severe pneumonia, causing shortness of breath and difficulty breathing.</p>

<p>Generally, coronavirus can cause more serious symptoms in people with weakened immune systems, the elderly, and people with chronic diseases such as diabetes, cancer, and chronic lung disease.</p>

<p>New coronavirus (COVID-19) is a new strain of coronavirus first identified in Wuhan, China.</p>

<p>On December 31, 2019, the world health organization (WHO) was informed of a group of cases of pneumonia of unknown cause found in the city of Wuhan, Hubei province, China.</p>

<p>On January 12, 2020, it was announced that a new coronavirus had been identified in samples obtained from patients, and initial analysis of the virus's genetic sequences suggested that this was the cause of the outbreak. This virus is called SARS-CoV-2, and the associated disease is COVID-19.</p>

<blockquote>As of Thursday, 19 March 2020, 1621 cases have been diagnosed in 227289 countries (including mainland China), with a total of 6,505 deaths. More than 9,000 cases and 130 deaths were reported in countries outside of mainland China. In China, 84% of cases reported to date are in Hubei province.</blockquote>

<p>Coronaviruses are a large family of viruses, some of which cause less severe diseases such as runny nose, and others cause more severe diseases such as middle East respiratory syndrome ( MERS ) and severe acute respiratory syndrome ( SARS) coronaviruses.</p>

<h2>Coronavirus research</h2>
<p>The coronavirus research group (CSG) international Committee on virus taxonomy has designated the etiological agent "severe acute respiratory syndrome coronavirus 2" ( SARS-coronavirus-2). Research on SARS-CoV-2 is ongoing. Initial information provided by China and who indicates that SARS-CoV-2 is a beta coronavirus that is genetically similar to SARS - like coronaviruses obtained from bats in Asia.</p>

<h2>Transmission of coronavirus</h2>
<p>The source of the flash has not been definitively determined. Preliminary studies revealed positive samples of SARS-CoV-2 at The huanan wholesale seafood market in Wuhan, however, some laboratory-confirmed patients did not report visiting this market. The zoonotic source of the outbreak has not yet been identified, but research is continuing.</p>

<p>The form of transmission is not known definitively, but information to date indicates that the coronavirus is transmitted from person to person. Therefore, precautions to prevent human-to-human transmission are appropriate for both patients with suspected coronavirus disease and confirmed cases.</p>
