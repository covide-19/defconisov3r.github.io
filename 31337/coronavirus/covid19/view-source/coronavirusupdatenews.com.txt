		<h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">Coronavirus: Live Update, News, Symptoms, how dangerous COVID-19</h1>	</header><!-- .entry-header -->

	<div class="entry-content clear" 
		itemprop="text"	>

		
		<h4>Corona Virus Live Update</h4>
<p>People from around the world are panicking and facing substantial restrictions on public life as workers are laid off, and amusement parks, schools, and social events are closed down. The Coronavirus crisis is <a href="https://www.ecdc.europa.eu/en/geographical-distribution-2019-ncov-cases">escalating across the world</a>, with over <strong><span style="color: #ff0000;">173,029</span></strong> reported cases and more than <span style="color: #ff0000;"><strong>6,663</strong></span> deaths and <strong><span style="color: #ff0000;">77,783</span></strong> are recovered globally according to the <a href="https://www.worldometers.info/coronavirus/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">worldometers</a>.</p>
<p>Apart from Antarctica, the novel COVID-19 has rolled out to the rest of the continents, with over 110 countries reporting confirmed cases of the virus. The World Health Organization (WHO) declared the outbreak of the virus an emergency of international concern, and the <a href="https://www.washingtonpost.com/health/coronavirus-forecasts-are-grim-its-going-to-get-worse/2020/03/11/2a177e0a-63b4-11ea-acca-80c22bbee96f_story.html">world continues to fight</a> one of the deadliest viruses on the planet.</p>
<ul>
<li>
<h4>What is a coronavirus?</h4>
</li>
</ul>
<p>A Coronavirus is a <a href="https://www.hopkinsmedicine.org/health/conditions-and-diseases/coronavirus">type of an infective agent</a> that is named for the spikes that project from their exteriors, similar to the sun’s corona. The virus that brings about respiratory illness comes from animals and can affect both animals and humans.</p>
<p>Scientists have discovered <a href="https://www.hopkinsmedicine.org/health/conditions-and-diseases/coronavirus">four kinds of coronaviruses</a>, that cause mild infections like the flu and the common cold. Other types of coronaviruses that have circulated before, include SARS, MERS, as well as SARS-CoV-2.</p>
<p>The novel virus, SARS-CoV-2 is the division of the Coronavirus family that caused COVID-19, the disease that started in Hubei province in Wuhan, China.</p>
<ul>
<li>
<h4>How does it spread?</h4>
</li>
</ul>
<p>COVID-19 <a href="https://www.health.nsw.gov.au/Infectious/alerts/Pages/coronavirus-faqs.aspx">spreads</a> from person-to-person, between people who are within 6 feet of each other, or through respiratory secretions released when an infected person sneezes or coughs without covering their mouth.</p>
<p>These secretions can land on the noses or mouths of unsuspecting victims who are close to the infected person, or the people can inhale the germs into their lungs. Similarly, the virus can spread from contact with contaminated objects and surfaces.</p>
<p>The spread of a virus can vary from person-to-person, with highly contagious viruses like measles spreading easily, and others not spreading as easily. In the case of COVID-19, <a href="https://www.health.nsw.gov.au/Infectious/alerts/Pages/coronavirus-faqs.aspx">the virus spreads easily in the affected areas</a>.</p>
<ul>
<li>
<h4>Watching out for symptoms</h4>
</li>
</ul>
<p>Based on what has been understood previously from other coronaviruses, <a href="https://www.who.int/health-topics/coronavirus">the following signs may start showing</a> 2 -14 days after contact with COVID-19: runny nose, fever, cough, persistent pain or pressure in the chest, sore throat, and shortness of breath.</p>
<p>While there is no vaccine to prevent the virus, the best way to protect yourself is to avoid getting exposed to the deadly virus, wash your hands with soap and water at all times, and call your doctor if you think you are developing the above symptoms. It&#8217;s also advisable to get a hand sanitizer to keep your hands germ-free.</p>
<ul>
<li>
<h4>How dangerous is it?</h4>
</li>
</ul>
<p>Although COVID-19 continues to spread at alarming rates, <a href="https://www.theguardian.com/world/2020/mar/12/coronavirus-symptoms-what-are-they-and-should-i-see-a-doctor">experts do not know how dangerous SARS-CoV-2 is</a>, and the danger may not be known until more information trickles in.</p>
<p>Nonetheless, statistics show that the virus affects middle-aged populaces, and children <a href="https://www.nytimes.com/2020/02/05/health/coronavirus-children.html">appear less likely</a> to contract the virus. <a href="https://www.nytimes.com/2020/02/20/health/coronavirus-men-women.html">People with weaker immune systems are more likely to die</a> from the Coronavirus infection compared to those with stronger immunity. Yet, what is worse than the disease, is the pure panic that the outbreak has spread among people.</p>
