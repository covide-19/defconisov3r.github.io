<h2 id="" class="wp-block-ro-header  h1 text-center">Frequently asked questions</h2>



<h1 class="wp-block-ro-header  h3 text-center">About Coronavirus (COVID-19)</h1>



<p class="wp-block-ro-paragraph spacing-block-mb-04 p-large has-grey-500-color text-center">All answers reviewed by our in-house medical team and authored by Rachel Kwon, MD.</p>



<p class="wp-block-ro-paragraph spacing-block-mb-07 p-small has-navy-100-color">Note: all the information relayed here is directly based on information and guidance from the CDC and WHO. This information is rapidly evolving, and it’s important to understand that there are still many unknowns about COVID-19 and how the disease spreads. Current knowledge and guidance is based in large part on what is known about similar viruses in the coronavirus family.</p>



<div class="wp-block-ro-accordion" data-single-open="true" data-accordion-group="true">
<div class="wp-block-ro-accordion-item" data-accordion="true"><div class="wp-block-ro-accordion-item__control" data-control="true"><h3>What is the difference between COVID-19 and coronavirus? What is SARS-CoV-2? What is novel coronavirus/nCoV?</h3></div><div class="wp-block-ro-accordion-item__content" data-content="true"><div class="wp-block-ro-accordion-item__content__inner">
<p class="wp-block-ro-paragraph  p"><strong>Coronavirus</strong> is the name of a family of viruses, some of which cause respiratory infections in humans. For example, there are many coronaviruses that cause the common cold. <strong>“Novel coronavirus</strong>,” abbreviated as <strong>nCoV</strong>, is a general term assigned to any newly-discovered coronavirus before giving it a formal name. <strong>SARS-CoV-2</strong> (previously called 2019-nCoV) is the formal name that the World Health Organization (WHO) has assigned to the coronavirus that causes <strong>COVID-19</strong>, the respiratory illness whose first case was identified in Wuhan, China in December 2019 (COVID-19 stands for Coronavirus Disease 2019). For simplicity, we’ll use the term “novel coronavirus” or “coronavirus” in these materials.<br><br><a href="https://www.cdc.gov/coronavirus/2019-nCoV/summary.html" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener" aria-label=" (opens in a new tab)">https://www.cdc.gov/coronavirus/2019-nCoV/summary.html</a></p>
</div></div></div>



<div class="wp-block-ro-accordion-item" data-accordion="true"><div class="wp-block-ro-accordion-item__control" data-control="true"><h3>What are the symptoms of COVID-19?</h3></div><div class="wp-block-ro-accordion-item__content" data-content="true"><div class="wp-block-ro-accordion-item__content__inner">
<p class="wp-block-ro-paragraph  p">COVID-19 is a respiratory infection, so typical symptoms are the same as any respiratory infection (like the common cold or flu): cough, fever/chills, and difficulty breathing. Symptoms can range from mild (most cases) to very severe, requiring hospitalization and the need for a ventilator (breathing machine). People with COVID-19 or any other respiratory infection might have any or all of the typical symptoms. There are some reports¹ of people later shown to have COVID-19 who initially had nausea or diarrhea but no respiratory symptoms, though these symptoms appear to be very uncommon in people with COVID-19.</p>



<p class="wp-block-ro-paragraph  p-small"><strong>¹</strong> <a href="https://www.thelancet.com/journals/lancet/article/PIIS0140-6736(20)30183-5/fulltext" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener" aria-label=" (opens in a new tab)">Huang C, Wang Y, Li X, et al. Clinical features of patients infected with 2019 novel coronavirus in Wuhan, China. Lancet. 2020;</a></p>
</div></div></div>



<div class="wp-block-ro-accordion-item" data-accordion="true"><div class="wp-block-ro-accordion-item__control" data-control="true"><h3>What should I do if I think I have symptoms of COVID-19?</h3></div><div class="wp-block-ro-accordion-item__content" data-content="true"><div class="wp-block-ro-accordion-item__content__inner">
<p class="wp-block-ro-paragraph  p">Unless you have recently traveled to one of the areas (a searchable list of countries is available on the <a href="https://wwwnc.cdc.gov/travel/notices" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener" aria-label=" (opens in a new tab)">CDC website</a>) with known cases of COVID-19 or had recent close contact with someone who has a known case of COVID-19, your symptoms are more likely to be due to the common cold or flu. However, viruses can spread quickly, and the best practices for avoiding spreading respiratory infections include staying at home if possible, washing your hands frequently and with proper technique, and consulting with a doctor or other qualified medical professional if your symptoms worsen or if you have other medical problems.<br><br>Note that information and guidance regarding COVID-19 are developing rapidly, and you should continue referring to the <a href="https://www.cdc.gov/coronavirus/2019-ncov/index.html">CDC website</a> to stay up to date on the latest developments.</p>
</div></div></div>



<div class="wp-block-ro-accordion-item" data-accordion="true"><div class="wp-block-ro-accordion-item__control" data-control="true"><h3>What should I do if I don’t have any symptoms, but I think I might have been exposed to novel coronavirus?</h3></div><div class="wp-block-ro-accordion-item__content" data-content="true"><div class="wp-block-ro-accordion-item__content__inner">
<p class="wp-block-ro-paragraph  p">According to the CDC at the time of this writing, exposure to novel coronavirus is possible if you have recently traveled to any of the areas with known cases of COVID-19 (a searchable list of countries is available on the <a href="https://wwwnc.cdc.gov/travel/notices" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener" aria-label=" (opens in a new tab)">CDC website</a>), or if you have been in close contact with someone who has a known case of COVID-19. If neither of the above is true, you are unlikely to have been exposed to novel coronavirus. However, viruses can spread quickly, so it is possible that at some point these criteria may change. If you think you have been exposed based on the current criteria and you don’t have any symptoms like cough, fever/chills, or difficulty breathing, the CDC recommends separating yourself from other people (quarantine) for 14 days from the last date you were potentially exposed, because 14 days is the longest incubation period seen for similar coronaviruses.<br><br>Note that information and guidance regarding COVID-19 are developing rapidly, and you should continue referring to the <a href="https://www.cdc.gov/coronavirus/2019-ncov/index.html">CDC website</a> to stay up to date on the latest developments.<br><br><a href="https://www.cdc.gov/coronavirus/2019-ncov/faq.html" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener" aria-label=" (opens in a new tab)">https://www.cdc.gov/coronavirus/2019-ncov/faq.html</a></p>
</div></div></div>



<div class="wp-block-ro-accordion-item" data-accordion="true"><div class="wp-block-ro-accordion-item__control" data-control="true"><h3>How does novel coronavirus spread? How can I minimize the risk of getting an infection or spreading infection to other people?</h3></div><div class="wp-block-ro-accordion-item__content" data-content="true"><div class="wp-block-ro-accordion-item__content__inner">
<p class="wp-block-ro-paragraph  p">Novel coronavirus is spread from person to person through respiratory droplets (from coughing, sneezing, or touching). The likelihood that a person will become infected with any virus after exposure is different for all viruses and is a spectrum. Novel coronavirus appears to be relatively easily spread from person to person. The CDC recommends the following best practices to help minimize the chances of spreading respiratory infections:<br>&#8211; Avoid close contact with people who are sick.<br>&#8211; Avoid touching your eyes, nose, and mouth.<br>&#8211; If you are sick, stay home.<br>&#8211; If you cough or sneeze, cover your mouth and nose with a tissue, then throw the tissue away and wash your hands.<br>&#8211; Clean and disinfect frequently touched objects and surfaces using regular household cleaning spray or cleaning wipe.<br>&#8211; Follow CDC’s recommendations for using a facemask:<br>    &#8211; CDC does <strong>not</strong> recommend that people who have no symptoms wear a facemask to protect themselves from respiratory diseases, including COVID-19.<br>    &#8211; People who show symptoms of COVID-19 should wear facemasks, in order to help prevent spreading the disease to others. Healthcare workers and people who are taking care of someone in close settings (like at home or in a health care facility) should also wear facemasks.<br>&#8211; Wash your hands frequently with soap and water for at least 20 seconds (one tip is to wash for the duration of singing the song “Happy Birthday” twice), especially after:<br>    &#8211; Using the bathroom<br>    &#8211; Before eating<br>    &#8211; After blowing your nose, coughing, or sneezing<br>&#8211; If you’re not near a sink with soap and water, an alcohol-based hand sanitizer with at least 60% alcohol can be used. Always wash with soap and water if your hands are visibly dirty.<br><br><a href="https://www.cdc.gov/coronavirus/2019-ncov/about/prevention-treatment.html" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener" aria-label=" (opens in a new tab)">https://www.cdc.gov/coronavirus/2019-ncov/about/prevention-treatment.html</a><br><br></p>
</div></div></div>



<div class="wp-block-ro-accordion-item" data-accordion="true"><div class="wp-block-ro-accordion-item__control" data-control="true"><h3>Can my pets get infected or infect me?</h3></div><div class="wp-block-ro-accordion-item__content" data-content="true"><div class="wp-block-ro-accordion-item__content__inner">
<p class="wp-block-ro-paragraph  p">Although novel coronavirus appears to have come from an animal source initially, the CDC states that “there is no reason to think that any animals including pets in the United States might be a source of infection with this new coronavirus.” Animals do sometimes spread other diseases to people, so washing your hands after being around animals is recommended in general.</p>
</div></div></div>



<div class="wp-block-ro-accordion-item" data-accordion="true"><div class="wp-block-ro-accordion-item__control" data-control="true"><h3>I’m an employer. Should I tell my employees to stay home?</h3></div><div class="wp-block-ro-accordion-item__content" data-content="true"><div class="wp-block-ro-accordion-item__content__inner">
<p class="wp-block-ro-paragraph  p">The CDC currently recommends the following to assess risk for COVID-19:<br>&#8211; Sick employees should be actively encouraged to stay home<br>&#8211; Employees who appear to have symptoms (fever, cough, shortness of breath) or develop symptoms at work should be separated from other employees and sent home immediately<br>&#8211; Employers should emphasize staying home when sick, as well as etiquette for coughing/sneezing and handwashing by all employees<br>&#8211; Routine environmental cleaning of frequently-touched surfaces like workstations, countertops, and doorknobs<br>&#8211; Employees who are planning to travel should be advised to check the latest <a href="https://wwwnc.cdc.gov/travel" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener" aria-label=" (opens in a new tab)">travel information</a> from the CDC and check for symptoms of respiratory illness before traveling<br><br>Additionally, risk assessments should be based on the above, not on race or country of origin.<br><br><a href="https://www.cdc.gov/coronavirus/2019-ncov/specific-groups/guidance-business-response.html" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener" aria-label=" (opens in a new tab)">https://www.cdc.gov/coronavirus/2019-ncov/specific-groups/guidance-business-response.html</a><br><br></p>
</div></div></div>



<div class="wp-block-ro-accordion-item" data-accordion="true"><div class="wp-block-ro-accordion-item__control" data-control="true"><h3>Is it safe to visit someone in the hospital, or to take a friend to their doctor’s office?</h3></div><div class="wp-block-ro-accordion-item__content" data-content="true"><div class="wp-block-ro-accordion-item__content__inner">
<p class="wp-block-ro-paragraph  p">You should use routine precautions when visiting people who are in the hospital or need to see a doctor for other reasons than a respiratory infection or respiratory symptoms. In general, it is a good idea to practice etiquette for coughing/sneezing and hand hygiene. Of course, for some people who are hospitalized for respiratory infections, special precautions may be required.</p>
</div></div></div>



<div class="wp-block-ro-accordion-item" data-accordion="true"><div class="wp-block-ro-accordion-item__control" data-control="true"><h3>Does wearing a mask or gloves protect me or those around me from getting infected with novel coronavirus?</h3></div><div class="wp-block-ro-accordion-item__content" data-content="true"><div class="wp-block-ro-accordion-item__content__inner">
<p class="wp-block-ro-paragraph  p">It is not currently recommended for people in a community setting (i.e., not a hospital or health care facility) without respiratory symptoms to wear a facemask to protect against the virus. For people in a community setting, wearing gloves is not more effective than practicing good hand hygiene (wash hands frequently with soap and water for at least 20 seconds). <br><br><a href="https://apps.who.int/iris/bitstream/handle/10665/331215/WHO-2019-nCov-IPCPPE_use-2020.1-eng.pdf" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener" aria-label=" (opens in a new tab)">https://apps.who.int/iris/bitstream/handle/10665/331215/WHO-2019-nCov-IPCPPE_use-2020.1-eng.pdf</a><br></p>
</div></div></div>



<div class="wp-block-ro-accordion-item" data-accordion="true"><div class="wp-block-ro-accordion-item__control" data-control="true"><h3>Is there a treatment, vaccine, or cure for COVID-19?</h3></div><div class="wp-block-ro-accordion-item__content" data-content="true"><div class="wp-block-ro-accordion-item__content__inner">
<p class="wp-block-ro-paragraph  p">There is no cure or specific antiviral treatment for COVID-19. The best treatment for COVID-19 (and other infections that don’t have a cure) is to relieve the symptoms. For mild symptoms, this usually involves rest, fluids, and medications to help with fever and cough as needed. People with severe symptoms (lots of trouble breathing, very high fever, inability to stay hydrated by drinking fluids by mouth) need hospitalization and potentially intensive care. Currently, there is no vaccine available to prevent COVID-19, and the best way to prevent exposure and illness is by using the techniques above (“How does novel coronavirus spread? How can I minimize the risk of getting an infection or spreading infection to other people?”).</p>
</div></div></div>
</div>



<h1 class="wp-block-ro-header spacing-block-mt-07 spacing-block-mb-06 h3 text-center">About Ro&#8217;s Coronavirus Telehealth Assessment</h1>



<div class="wp-block-ro-accordion" data-single-open="true" data-accordion-group="true">
<div class="wp-block-ro-accordion-item" data-accordion="true"><div class="wp-block-ro-accordion-item__control" data-control="true"><h3>What is Ro’s Coronavirus Telehealth Assessment?</h3></div><div class="wp-block-ro-accordion-item__content" data-content="true"><div class="wp-block-ro-accordion-item__content__inner">
<p>Ro has created a free telehealth triage service for people who are seeking guidance and information about the novel coronavirus (COVID-19).</p>



<p>If an individual thinks they may be experiencing signs or symptoms of the novel coronavirus (COVID-19), they complete Ro’s online assessment and, if appropriate, we’ll connect them with a doctor for a free video consultation.</p>
</div></div></div>



<div class="wp-block-ro-accordion-item" data-accordion="true"><div class="wp-block-ro-accordion-item__control" data-control="true"><h3>How does it work?</h3></div><div class="wp-block-ro-accordion-item__content" data-content="true"><div class="wp-block-ro-accordion-item__content__inner">
<p>Developed with guidance from infectious disease specialists, Ro’s telehealth assessment collects self-reported health information (e.g., health history, symptoms, travel history, locale, proximity to confirmed cases). Based on that information and using the most up-to-date guidelines from the Centers for Disease Control (CDC), the World Health Organization (WHO), and state/local public health departments, we provide an initial risk assessment.</p>



<p>Those who are symptomatic or have other risk factors (e.g., travel history or proximity to confirmed cases, pre-existing/underlying conditions) will be connected to a licensed physician for a free video consultation.</p>



<p>During the video consultation, the doctor will collect additional information about the patient’s symptoms and concerns, recommend the proper course of action (which may include self-quarantine), and, if recommended, instruct them to follow up with a local provider for testing and diagnosis. For patients who are recommended to seek in-person care, we will provide them resources to find a local provider so they can get the help they need. We also report suspected cases to the applicable public health authorities, as required, to help inform local and national response efforts.</p>



<p>Individuals who are at low risk of having contracted COVID-19 will be guided to a number of resources at our&nbsp;<a rel="noreferrer noopener" href="https://www.getroman.com/health-guide/coronavirus/" target="_blank">Coronavirus </a><a href="https://www.getroman.com/health-guide/coronavirus/" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener" aria-label="Resource (opens in a new tab)">Resource</a><a rel="noreferrer noopener" href="https://www.getroman.com/health-guide/coronavirus/" target="_blank"> Hub</a>&nbsp;and will be encouraged to keep monitoring their symptoms. These resources are regularly updated and are based on the latest government guidelines and best practices. Our goal is that these resources will help the public understand this complex virus and help keep themselves, their families, and their communities safe.</p>
</div></div></div>



<div class="wp-block-ro-accordion-item" data-accordion="true"><div class="wp-block-ro-accordion-item__control" data-control="true"><h3>Where is the assessment available?</h3></div><div class="wp-block-ro-accordion-item__content" data-content="true"><div class="wp-block-ro-accordion-item__content__inner">
<p>We’ve launched the assessment to people located in the state of Washington and will expand to to additional states as quickly as we can as the national response to COVID-19 develops.</p>
</div></div></div>



<div class="wp-block-ro-accordion-item" data-accordion="true"><div class="wp-block-ro-accordion-item__control" data-control="true"><h3>Why are you doing this?</h3></div><div class="wp-block-ro-accordion-item__content" data-content="true"><div class="wp-block-ro-accordion-item__content__inner">
<p>We want to do everything we can to provide assistance to people who are concerned about COVID-19. Ro’s telehealth platform is uniquely suited to help in a number of ways:</p>



<p><strong>Unburdening hospitals and clinics</strong>.</p>



<p>We have consulted with public health experts who are worried that coronavirus fears will overcrowd and overburden our health system, making it more challenging and costly for people who need in-person or emergency care to find it.</p>



<p>We’re hopeful that Ro’s triage system will reduce strain on providers who are treating higher-risk and sicker patients.</p>



<p><strong>Advising symptomatic patients</strong></p>



<p>We expect that people suffering from the common cold or the flu will be concerned about COVID-19. Initially talking to a remote doctor via telehealth, as the CDC has recommended, can provide comfort and guidance on how to address their symptoms, get healthy, and reduce the spread to others. For those at risk of COVID-19, Ro-affiliated doctors can advise them on appropriate next steps, whether that’s to self-quarantine or seek additional care.</p>



<p><strong>Providing trusted health information</strong>.</p>



<p>As more cases are reported, we expect public fears to increase and misinformation to spread. We believe that providing people with easily-accessible, updated information about COVID-19 will help calm their concerns. For people who don’t have an established provider and aren’t sure where to turn, Ro can be an initial entry point into the healthcare system.</p>
</div></div></div>



<div class="wp-block-ro-accordion-item" data-accordion="true"><div class="wp-block-ro-accordion-item__control" data-control="true"><h3>Are doctors diagnosing or testing for COVID-19 on your platform?</h3></div><div class="wp-block-ro-accordion-item__content" data-content="true"><div class="wp-block-ro-accordion-item__content__inner">
<p>No. This is a triage program that will allow people to easily access a risk assessment tool, and if appropriate, a doctor consultation at no cost. We are not diagnosing or testing for COVID-19.</p>
</div></div></div>



<div class="wp-block-ro-accordion-item" data-accordion="true"><div class="wp-block-ro-accordion-item__control" data-control="true"><h3>Does Ro have access to approved COVID-19 tests?</h3></div><div class="wp-block-ro-accordion-item__content" data-content="true"><div class="wp-block-ro-accordion-item__content__inner">
<p>No. Ro is not providing testing for COVID-19.</p>
</div></div></div>



<div class="wp-block-ro-accordion-item" data-accordion="true"><div class="wp-block-ro-accordion-item__control" data-control="true"><h3>Will Ro provide consultation to caregivers for those who may be at-risk and unable to use the platform (e.g., elderly, children)?</h3></div><div class="wp-block-ro-accordion-item__content" data-content="true"><div class="wp-block-ro-accordion-item__content__inner">
