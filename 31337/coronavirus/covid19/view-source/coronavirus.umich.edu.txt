<h1 class="postTitle">2019 Novel Coronavirus COVID-19</h1>
<p><em>Updated March 16, 2020</em></p>
<p>University of Michigan health professionals have been closely monitoring the spread of COVID-19 over the past two months.</p>
<p>Our goals are to deliver on our mission while protecting health and safety by minimizing the potential spread of the disease, both within our community and in the broader society. The changes we have implemented follow the general public health principle of limiting the interaction of people within larger groups to diminish disease transmission and protect everyone in our communities, particularly the most vulnerable.</p>
<p><strong>Important COVID-19 updates:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Classes <b>are being offered</b> in alternative formats – not meeting in person through April 21, the last day of classes this semester.</li>
<li><strong>All U-M events</strong>, and events organized by others on our campuses, are <strong>canceled</strong> until at least April 21.</li>
<li>All <strong>commencement</strong> ceremonies are <strong>being postponed to a future date</strong>.</li>
<li>All <strong>exams</strong> will take place <strong>remotely</strong>. Students are encouraged to go home to their permanent residence.</li>
<li>U-M <strong>libraries and museums and workout facilities are closed</strong> until further notice.</li>
<li>MDining is offering <strong>boxed meals</strong> during specific hours of the day.</li>
<li>U-M is <strong>suspending</strong> all international travel until at least April 21, with rare exceptions requiring approval. Domestic travel for university business is strongly discouraged.</li>
<li>All U-M education abroad programs will be <strong>altered or suspended</strong>.</li>
<li><strong>Avoid</strong> gathering socially in large groups.</li>
<li>Members of the U-M community <strong>have tested positive</strong> for COVID-19. <a href="https://www.washtenaw.org/3095/COVID-19">The Washtenaw County Health Department</a> is the source of updates and information on positive cases in the county.</li>
</ul>
<p>This is a rapidly evolving situation. U-M officials and medical experts, in close coordination with the state and local public health experts, are closely monitoring for developments and will offer additional guidance to the university community as soon as it is available.</p>
<p>Read the latest message from President Schlissel on final exams, commencement and flexible work arrangements <a href="https://president.umich.edu/news-communications/statements/important-updates-on-finals-commencements-flexible-work-arrangements-and-additional-covid-19-announcements/">here</a>.</p>
<p>This site will be updated as additional guidance becomes available.</p>
