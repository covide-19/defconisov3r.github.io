class="wp-block-media-text__content"><h4>About the Project</h4><p><em>&#8220;COME FOR THE MISINFORMATION, STAY FOR THE FACTS&#8221; </em></p><p>COVID19MisInfo.org is a <strong>Rapid Response Project</strong> of the <a
href="https://socialmedialab.ca/">Ryerson Social Media Lab</a> at Ted Rogers School of Management. The aim of this project is two-fold: (1) put a spotlight on COVID-19 related misinformation and (2) to provide Canadians with timely and actionable information that we all can use to protect ourselves and our communities. <a
href="https://covid19misinfo.org/flattenthecurve/">#flattenthecurve</a></p><p>The project website is a work in progress. Our team of computational social scientists, communications professionals and developers are hard at work <a
rel="noreferrer noopener" aria-label="curating trusted information about COVID-19 (opens in a new tab)" href="https://covid19misinfo.org/covid19-resources/" target="_blank">curating trusted information about COVID-19</a> and  developing additional real-time information visualization dashboards to keep track of false claims related to COVID-19 from around the web.</p></div></div><hr

rel="noreferrer noopener" href="https://www.ryerson.ca/" target="_blank">Ryerson University</a>.&nbsp;The lab studies how social media is changing the way people and organizations communicate, share information, conduct business and form communities online, and how these changes impact society.&nbsp;The&nbsp;<a
rel="noreferrer noopener" aria-label=" (opens in a new tab)" href="https://socialmedialab.ca/research/" target="_blank">lab’s research&nbsp;</a>seeks to advance the public’s understanding of the benefits and pitfalls of social media adoption.</p></div><div
