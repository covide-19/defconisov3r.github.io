<p><strong>Stop The Coronavirus</strong></p>



<p><strong>Background –</strong> The <strong>coronavirus</strong> started in China in Wuhan. Experts believe that it was passed from animals to humans in the wet markets in Wuhan, China. These markets are called wet markets because sometimes the traders kill the animals in front of the customers.  These markets have different types of animals and mammals close together in cages, some animals would be bleeding or have bodily fluids leaking down on top of other mammals in cages below. The theory is that this is how the virus started and spread into humans.&nbsp; There is much debate in relation to how and where the virus started. In December 2019, the virus started to appear in China. The <strong>Coronavirus spread</strong> very quickly over a few weeks. It has spread into Europe and the United States. The <strong>Coronavirus </strong>have also spread in South America and Africa.</p>



<p><strong>Coronaviruses </strong>are a group of virus that mainly infect mammals and birds. The virus causes respiratory problems and common cold like symptoms. Other forms of the virus like <strong>SARS, MERS</strong> and this new coronavirus <strong>COVID-19</strong> can be lethal to humans. There are no vaccines or antiviral drugs to date to treat human <strong>coronavirus.</strong> </p>



<p>Some countries have gone into lockdown to stop the spread of the virus. <strong>China locked down Wuhan with a population of 11 million people. </strong>Italy has ordered simple measures.</p>



<p></p>



<p><strong>Isolate and Stay at Home –</strong></p>



<p><strong>Advice –</strong> Many Governments have advised to
isolate and stop mass gathering. Avoid close contact with others. <strong>Social distancing</strong> is the advice. This
is required to <strong>stop the</strong> <strong>virus spreading</strong>. China done this in
Wuhan on a mass scale, it appeared to work and contain the virus. Some
governments have been slow to act, concerned about their economies. This is not
the correct approach because this virus will stress and could affect the world
economy in a very negative way. To stop the spread of <strong>coronavirus </strong>we need extreme measures.&nbsp; This is up to the people and citizens of each
country to stop the virus spreading.</p>



<p>Obey health advice from the <strong><a href="https://www.who.int/emergencies/diseases/novel-coronavirus-2019" class="rank-math-link" target="_blank" rel="noopener">WHO World Health Organization</a></strong>. Isolate from other members of the public. <strong>Avoid all non essential travel.</strong></p>



<p></p>



<p><strong>STAY AT HOME IS THE MESSAGE</strong></p>



<p><strong>Tips for Stopping the Spread of the
Conronavirus – </strong></p>



<p>1 – Wash
your hands regularly for 20 seconds or more.</p>



<p>2 – Use anti
bacterial hand wash.</p>



<p>3 – Use hand
sanitizer where possible. </p>



<p>3 – Don’t
panic, we will get through this.</p>



<p>4 – Practice
good sneeze and cough methods, use tissues where possible.</p>



<p>5 – Avoid
touching your face, nose and eyes.</p>



<p>6 – Disinfect items you regularly touch.</p>



<p>7 – If it helps you wear a mask, or you can make a mask from hand wipes. <a href="https://m.facebook.com/story.php?story_fbid=739363203262443&amp;id=100015663097949&amp;sfnsn=mo&amp;d=n&amp;vh=i" class="rank-math-link" target="_blank" rel="noopener">(See link of video here)</a></p>



<p>8 – Obey
good social distancing, don’t shake hands, no hugs or kisses.</p>



<p>9 – Stay
away from large gatherings, pubs, hotels, retails stores, concerts etc.</p>



<p>10 – Avoid
all non essential travel, in your car, to airports etc. </p>



<p>11 – Obey
health advice from your Government.</p>



<p>12 – Ask your employer if you can work from home, depending on what you do for a living.</p>



<p>13 – Avoid
all public transport.</p>



<p>14 – Avoid
social interactions with friends and family, contact them by phone, via Wats
APP, Facebook messenger.</p>



<p>15 –
Continue to work if at all possible.</p>



<p>16 – Do not leave your home unless you HAVE TO. </p>



<p></p>



<p><strong>Symptoms – </strong></p>



<p>Health experts have said it can take up to <strong>14 days</strong> for the symptoms of <strong>Coronavirus</strong> to appear so keep this in mind. It may be a good idea to keep a log of who you are in contact with on a daily basis. You could make a note of all people on your smartphone or with a biro and a piece of paper. You could even keep a note of their contact details also. </p>



<p></p>



<p><strong>Symptoms of COVID-19 Coronavirus – </strong></p>



<p>1 – A cough,
anytype of cough.</p>



<p>2 – Difficulty
breathing.</p>



<p>3 – High
temperatures (especially over 38 Celsius)</p>



<p>4 – Shortness
of breath.</p>



<p>5 – Fatigue.</p>



<p>6 –
Headaches.</p>



<p>7 – Sore
throat.</p>



<p>8 – Aches
and pains.</p>



<p><strong>If you feel you have some of these symptoms, contact your doctor and take medical advice and self isolate if advised</strong>.</p>



<p><strong>You must isolate from other family members also straight away if advised.</strong></p>



<p><strong>All other people in close with you should also self isolate </strong></p>



<p></p>



<p><strong>Why it so important – </strong></p>



<p>The <strong>COVID-19 virus</strong> is highly a contagious virus. It is spreading all over the world. Over the past few months it has become a world health emergency. It can be lethal to people with underlying conditions like diabetes, the elderly and cancer patients. In some countries confirmed cases are increasing by 30% a day which is very high. <strong>COVID-19 coronavirus</strong> is an <strong>epidemic </strong>like we haven’t seen before. This virus is deadly and experts believe it’s 20 times more deadly than the common flu. The need for widespread testing will require huge amounts of people to become in contact with health care professionals which could cause the virus to spread further. </p>



<p></p>



<p><strong>Serve Symptoms of COVID-19
Coronavirus – </strong></p>



<p>1 – Respiratory
failure</p>



<p>2 – Pneumonia&nbsp;</p>



<p>2 – Low
blood oxygen saturation </p>



<p>3 –
Intensive care admission </p>



<p>Experts have said that <strong>15 to 20% could suffer from severe symptoms</strong>, this will put massive pressure on our health systems. Around 80% of people infected will just have the mild symptoms. </p>



<p>People suffering with severe symptoms will need  intensive respiratory medical treatment and this will be challenging for our health systems. Some people will need special care to recover from <strong>COVID-19</strong>. If the accounts of 30% increasing day on day is correct, very challenging time are ahead. There will be a huge demand for ventilators and respiratory equipment all over the world.  </p>



<p><strong>No vaccine, </strong>and<strong> no cure</strong> which make this <strong>coronavirus</strong>
extremely dangerous to world health. It will take time to develop a vaccine.
The virus is new in terms of just a few months old and immunity is still a huge
issue.&nbsp; The numbers of new confirmed
cases appearing worldwide is very concerning.</p>
