<h2>Why is Covid-19 a Problem?</h2>



<p>There are several concerns regarding Covid-19, the Coronavirus, or the Chinese Wuhan Virus.  According to the CDC, these are:</p>



<ol><li>It is a respiratory-borne illness.</li><li>It can be transmitted through personal contact.</li><li>It may be transmitted through touching surfaces infected with the virus.</li><li>It appears to be spreading easily and sustainably throughout various communities.</li><li>It can be spread when those infected are asymptomatic. In other words, we cannot tell people to stay home if they&#8217;re feeling ill to stymie the virus because people may not feel ill at all.</li></ol>



<h2>Should I worry?</h2>



<p>The short answer is yes. You should worry.  However, your worry should lead to prudence and not panic.</p>



<h3>What does prudence look like?</h3>



<p>Some examples of prudence might be:</p>



<ul><li>Frequently wash your hands with soap and warm water for at least 20 seconds, especially after coming into contact with people or public places.</li><li>When you wash your hands, make sure to scrub between your fingers, under your fingernails, and the backs of your hands as well as your palms.</li><li>Don&#8217;t shake hands. Wave.</li><li>Don&#8217;t touch your face when your hands aren&#8217;t clean.</li><li>Avoid public places, large crowds, and other community activities.</li><li>Stay home if you&#8217;re not feeling well.</li><li>Stay home if someone in your house isn&#8217;t feeling well.</li><li>Getting tested.</li><li>Seek medical help if you contract the illness; however, you should call your local hospital first and find out what their protocol is for receiving you as a patient. Don&#8217;t just walk into the emergency room and infect everyone there.</li></ul>



<h3>What does panic look like?</h3>



<p>Some example of panic are:</p>



<ul><li>Hoarding toilet paper and other non-perishables.</li><li>Buying over-priced products from those who hoarded products for the sole purpose of making money on your panic.</li><li>Fighting with others over retail products.</li><li>Attacking Asian people and blaming them for spreading the disease. Not only is this wrong but a lot of Asian people are not Chinese.</li><li>Looting.</li><li>Walking into the emergency room unannounced when you feel ill. There is no reason to infect everyone else. Call your local hospital first and ask them what their protocol is for dealing with patients who have the virus.</li></ul>



<h2>Beating the China Virus</h2>



<p>While the Chinese Wuhan Virus spreads easily and can be deadly in some cases, it is important for us to not panic. The U.S. is taking measures to control the virus and the best thing we can do is cooperate, take precautions, and to be considerate of one another.</p>
