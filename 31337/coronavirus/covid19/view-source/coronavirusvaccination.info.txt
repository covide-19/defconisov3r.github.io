<p>After March 11th when the <a href="https://www.who.int/" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener" aria-label="WHO (opens in a new tab)">WHO</a> has declared COVID-19 a <a href="https://time.com/5791661/who-coronavirus-pandemic-declaration/" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener" aria-label="worldwide global pandemic (opens in a new tab)">worldwide global pandemic</a>, the world will never be the same.</p>



<p>In years to come we will witness global changes in public health sectors across the globe. We can also expect many changes in governmental and economic sectors which will affect public and social life of each and every individual.</p>



<p>The novel coronavirus (COVID-19) pandemic has reminded us all how very dependent we are from health care organisations and pharmaceutical industry, which is still under-developed in many world countries in the midst of the 21st century.</p>



<p>The situation we faced has clearly illustrated we live in one world and we are all inter-connected. We are facing a global problem and we have to work to find a solution together. Facing the virus we had to step together and obey the rules and guidelines of self-isolation, not only for our own safety but for that of our brothers and sisters, neighbors and friends. Those in our vicinity as well as those living on the other side of the globe.</p>



<p>Before we take a look at the vaccination program, safety and procedures, let&#8217;s take a broader look at the situation to understand why worldwide global immunization is one of the main prevention mechanisms.</p>



<h2>Could the pandemic have been prevented?</h2>



<h3>Coronavirus Predictions and Warnings</h3>

<p>Luckily, the world-famous billionaire was prepared. Bill and Melinda Gates foundation which is funding various humanitarian actions and organizations around the world have already <a rel="noreferrer noopener" aria-label=" (opens in a new tab)" href="https://www.gatesfoundation.org/TheOptimist/coronavirus" target="_blank">raised and contributed millions of dollars</a> to speed the development of the vaccine and help with other prevention and treatment measures.</p>
