            <div id="faqs" class="faq-block">

            <h3>Frequently asked questions</h3>
        
        
    </div>
    
        <div class="faq-accordion">
            
                <div tabindex="0" class="faq-item pb-2 mb-4 toggle-faqcontent" role="button" aria-pressed="false" aria-expanded="false" aria-controls="faq-content-1">
    <div class="faq-question-container flex justify-between">
        <h4 class="faq-question inline-block">What is COVID-19 (novel coronavirus)? </h4>
        <div class="faq-question--icon faq-question--icon-plus pt-3">
            <i class="gg-math-plus"></i>
        </div>
        <div class="faq-question--icon faq-question--icon-minus pt-3">
            <i class="gg-math-minus"></i>
        </div>
    </div>
    <div id="faq-content-1" aria-hidden="true" class="faq-content">
        <p><span>Covid-19 is a new type of coronavirus disease. Coronaviruses are a large and diverse family of viruses which cause illnesses such as the common cold. </span></p><p><span>Also known as severe acute respiratory syndrome coronavirus 2 (SARS-CoV-2) or 2019-nCoV, it was identified by Chinese authorities on 7 January 2020. It had not previously been detected in humans or animals. Laboratory testing ruled out other respiratory pathogens such as influenza, avian influenza, adenovirus, and the SARS and MERS coronaviruses.<br></span></p>
    </div>
</div>

            
                <div tabindex="0" class="faq-item pb-2 mb-4 toggle-faqcontent" role="button" aria-pressed="false" aria-expanded="false" aria-controls="faq-content-2">
    <div class="faq-question-container flex justify-between">
        <h4 class="faq-question inline-block">What are the symptoms of COVID-19? </h4>
        <div class="faq-question--icon faq-question--icon-plus pt-3">
            <i class="gg-math-plus"></i>
        </div>
        <div class="faq-question--icon faq-question--icon-minus pt-3">
            <i class="gg-math-minus"></i>
        </div>
    </div>
    <div id="faq-content-2" aria-hidden="true" class="faq-content">
        <p><span>Symptoms include a fever, coughing and shortness of breath. Symptoms of COVID-19 are similar to a range of other illnesses such as influenza and do not necessarily mean that you have COVID-19. <br><br>If you have these symptoms call Healthline (for free) on 0800 358 5453 (or +64 9 358 5453 for international SIMs) or your local doctor for the most up to date professional advice. <br><br>If you are severely unwell, for example having trouble breathing, contact emergency services .</span></p>
    </div>
</div>

            
                <div tabindex="0" class="faq-item pb-2 mb-4 toggle-faqcontent" role="button" aria-pressed="false" aria-expanded="false" aria-controls="faq-content-3">
    <div class="faq-question-container flex justify-between">
        <h4 class="faq-question inline-block">How does COVID-19 spread? </h4>
        <div class="faq-question--icon faq-question--icon-plus pt-3">
            <i class="gg-math-plus"></i>
        </div>
        <div class="faq-question--icon faq-question--icon-minus pt-3">
            <i class="gg-math-minus"></i>
        </div>
    </div>
    <div id="faq-content-3" aria-hidden="true" class="faq-content">
        <p><span>Like the flu, COVID-19 can be transmitted from person to person. The scientific evidence confirms that COVID-19 is spread by droplets. This means that when an infected person coughs, sneezes or talks, they may generate droplets containing the virus. These droplets are too large to stay in the air for long, so they quickly settle on surrounding surfaces.</span><span>&nbsp;</span></p><p><span>Droplet-spread diseases can be spread by:</span><span>&nbsp;</span></p><ul> <li><span></span><span> </span><span> </span><span>coughing and sneezing</span><span></span></li> <li><span></span><span> </span><span> </span><span>close personal contact</span><span></span></li> <li><span></span><span> </span><span> </span><span>contact with an object or surface with viral&nbsp;droplets on it and then touching your mouth, nose or eyes.</span><span></span></li> </ul><p><span>That's why it's&nbsp;really important&nbsp;to practice good hygiene, regularly wash and thoroughly dry your hands</span><span>,</span><span>&nbsp;and practice good etiquette</span><span>&nbsp;by coughing or sneezing into your elbow.</span><span>&nbsp;</span></p>
    </div>
</div>

            
                <div tabindex="0" class="faq-item pb-2 mb-4 toggle-faqcontent" role="button" aria-pressed="false" aria-expanded="false" aria-controls="faq-content-4">
    <div class="faq-question-container flex justify-between">
        <h4 class="faq-question inline-block">How do I protect myself and others from COVID-19? </h4>
        <div class="faq-question--icon faq-question--icon-plus pt-3">
            <i class="gg-math-plus"></i>
        </div>
        <div class="faq-question--icon faq-question--icon-minus pt-3">
            <i class="gg-math-minus"></i>
        </div>
    </div>
    <div id="faq-content-4" aria-hidden="true" class="faq-content">
        <p><span>You should always practice good hygiene by:</span><span>&nbsp;</span></p><ul> <li><span></span><span> </span><span> </span><span>covering coughs and sneezes with disposable tissues</span><span></span></li> <li><span></span><span> </span><span> </span><span>washing hands for at least 20 seconds with water and soap</span><span>,&nbsp;</span><span>and drying them thoroughly:</span><span></span> <ul> <li><span></span><span> </span><span> </span><span>before eating or handling food</span><span></span></li> <li><span></span><span> </span><span> </span><span>after using the toilet</span><span></span></li> <li><span></span><span> </span><span> </span><span>after coughing, sneezing, blowing your nose or wiping children’s noses</span><span></span></li> <li><span></span><span> </span><span> </span><span>after caring for sick people.</span><span></span></li> </ul> </li> </ul><p><span>People with symptoms of acute respiratory infection should practice good cough etiquette (maintain distance, cover coughs and sneezes with disposable tissues, and wash hands). If you have concerns, you can contact the dedicated COVID-19 Healthline for free on </span><strong>0800 358 5453.</strong></p>
    </div>
</div>

            
                <div tabindex="0" class="faq-item pb-2 mb-4 toggle-faqcontent" role="button" aria-pressed="false" aria-expanded="false" aria-controls="faq-content-5">
    <div class="faq-question-container flex justify-between">
        <h4 class="faq-question inline-block">What should I do if I am immune-compromised or have immune-compromised children or whānau?</h4>
        <div class="faq-question--icon faq-question--icon-plus pt-3">
            <i class="gg-math-plus"></i>
        </div>
        <div class="faq-question--icon faq-question--icon-minus pt-3">
            <i class="gg-math-minus"></i>
        </div>
    </div>
    <div id="faq-content-5" aria-hidden="true" class="faq-content">
        <p>People with underlying medical conditions, such as a compromised immune system, liver disease, cancer, kidney disease, heart disease and diabetes mellitus, need to take more precautions to protect themselves against all infections, including COVID-19.&nbsp;</p><p>While New Zealand currently has very few cases of COVID-19,&nbsp;and no evidence of sustained person-to-person transmission in our communities, we recommend people take the following simple steps to protect yourself and others:&nbsp;</p><ul> <li>Avoid close contact with people with cold or flu-like illnesses.</li> <li>Cover coughs and sneezes with disposable tissues or clothing.</li> <li>Wash hands for at least 20 seconds with water and soap and dry them thoroughly: <ul> <li>before eating or handling food</li> <li>after using the toilet</li> <li>after coughing, sneezing, blowing your nose or wiping children’s noses</li> <li>after caring for sick people. </li> </ul> </li> </ul><p> &nbsp;Additional measures that you and your&nbsp;whānau&nbsp;and friends can take include:&nbsp;&nbsp;</p><ul> <li>If you are immune-compromised, avoid staying with a person who is self-isolating (because they are a close contact of a confirmed case of COVID-19 or have recently travelled to any country except those listed in the countries and areas of concern under Category 2.)</li> <li>You should stay at least 2 metres away from people who are unwell, if you are immune-compromised.</li> <li>It's also important that everyone helps to protect the safety of immunocompromised people living in our community. For example, if you’re unwell, avoid contact with someone who is immune-compromised.</li> <li>We recommend checking <a href="https://www.safetravel.govt.nz/news/novel-coronavirus-covid-19">safe travel advice</a> about COVID-19 if you plan overseas travel.</li> <li>At this time, it wouldn’t make sense for someone who is immune-compromised to wear a mask when in public to decrease risk for catching COVID-19. However, if your health care provider advises you to wear a mask when in public areas because you have a particularly vulnerable immune system, follow that advice. </li> <li>If you are taking immunosuppressive drugs we advise that you do not stop this medication without first consulting your GP or specialist.&nbsp;&nbsp;</li> </ul><p><a title="See a list of Category 2 countries on the health.govt.nz website&nbsp;" rel="noopener noreferrer" href="https://www.health.govt.nz/our-work/diseases-and-conditions/covid-19-novel-coronavirus/covid-19-novel-coronavirus-health-advice-general-public/covid-19-countries-and-areas-concern" target="_blank">See a list of Category 2 countries on the health.govt.nz website&nbsp;</a></p>
